This project analyses user engagement and retention patterns for the Google Merchandise Store using Google Analytics 4 (GA4) demo data. The analysis simulates a real-world business scenario where a data analyst is tasked with understanding user behaviour, identifying drop-off points, and recommending strategies to improve retention.
Tools Used

Google Analytics 4 (GA4) — data extraction and exploration
Microsoft Power BI — dashboard and visualisation
Microsoft Excel — data preparation

Data Source
Google Merchandise Store GA4 Demo Account — publicly available dataset provided by Google for analytics learning and portfolio purposes.
Business Questions Answered

Are established (returning) users more engaged than new users?
Which device category drives the highest engagement?
Which traffic source brings the most valuable users?

Key Findings
1. New vs Established Users

Established users show a 70.4% engagement rate vs only 30.1% for new users
Despite lower volume, established users spend 3x longer on the site (4m 58s vs 1m 39s)
A spike of 110% in new users was detected on 22nd February 2026, likely driven by a promotional campaign
The store is heavily acquisition-dependent, constantly spending on attracting new users rather than retaining existing ones

2. Device Category Analysis

Mobile drives 55% of total sessions but has a critically low engagement rate of only 15.3%
Desktop users show 59.1% engagement rate and spend 4m 28s on average per session
This indicates the site is not optimised for mobile users, causing high drop-off rates
Tablet users show 50.6% engagement, suggesting mid-screen devices perform better than mobile

3. Traffic Source Analysis

Organic Shopping drives the highest engagement rate at 85% — users arriving via Google Shopping are high-intent buyers
Referral traffic shows the longest average session duration at 5m 27s, indicating word-of-mouth brings the most valuable users
Direct traffic has the highest session volume (67,112) but surprisingly low engagement at 23.2%
Email campaigns show strong engagement at 77.1% despite low volume, suggesting untapped potential

Recommendations

Invest in retention strategies — loyalty programmes, personalised offers, and email campaigns to convert one-time visitors into returning customers
Prioritise mobile UX optimisation — simplify navigation, improve load times, and streamline mobile checkout to convert high mobile traffic into engagement
Scale referral and organic shopping channels — these bring the highest quality users with the best engagement rates
Replicate campaign success — analyse the February 22nd spike to understand what drove it and replicate those conditions
